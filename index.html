General Understanding of Triage Categories

Urgent (Categories 1–3): Life-threatening emergencies, severe chest pain, major trauma, or difficulty breathing. These cases are always prioritised by clinical staff first.

Minor (Categories 4–5): Non-life-threatening conditions such as sprains, minor cuts, or viral symptoms. Waiting times are typically longer as resources are directed to more critical patients.

Note: These estimates are statistical models and do not replace professional medical triage. Actual wait times may vary.
